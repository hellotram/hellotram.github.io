Childhood critical illnesses have been on the rise

In America, 13,000 children are diagnosed with cancer each year, 200,000 children live with either type 1 or type 2 diabetes, and 9 million children suffer from asthma.

With that rising number of cases, cost of treatment and care are also extremely high. Sadly, a majority of patients come from lower income, minority, and uninsured families. Even if they are insured, that doesn't mean they can afford paying the flood of medical bills coming their way.

Financially, it was a disaster coming. Her first hospital bill, not including surgery and anesthesia, was $308,000.00.

-Kellie, whose daughter has a serious disability

Our Proposal

Navicare, the bridge between families of kids with critical illnesses and assistance resources.

Parents and guardians of kids with chronic conditions may feel overwhelmed by the cost and demands of medical care and may lack the resources to seek out assistance.

Having all those concerns in mind, we proudly proposed Navicare, an app that could help families of children with critical illnesses find the right assistance resources they need, set up the first steps of enrolling and keep track of their progresses.
