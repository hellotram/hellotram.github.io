Childhood critical illnesses have been on the rise

There has been a significant increase in children in recent decades - up 27% since 1975 in kids under age 19.

Families of these children, once hit with the news, have to cope with different kinds of problems - treatment, household bills, transportation, and more.

The process of finding and asking for help from multiple organizations is oftentimes tedious and repetitive.

That elongates the treatment time and worsens the situation.
